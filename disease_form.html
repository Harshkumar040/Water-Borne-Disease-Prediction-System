Water Quality & Disease Risk Factors

The system evaluates three primary parameters to estimate waterborne disease risk: pH imbalance can cause heavy metal leaching, turbidity indicates suspended particles that harbor pathogens, and temperature influences microbial growth (especially Vibrio cholerae, E. coli).

🔄 pH Scale
Acidic (<6.5): corrosion, metal release. Alkaline (>8.5): disinfection inefficiency.
🌪️ Turbidity effect
>5 NTU shields pathogens from UV/chlorine. High values linked to gastroenteritis.
🔥 Temperature risk
25°C–40°C accelerates bacterial replication. Cooler water reduces risk.
