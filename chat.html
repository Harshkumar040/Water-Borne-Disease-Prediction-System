Health Information Hub

Water Safety Tips

Always boil water for 1 minute, store in clean containers, and use proper filtration methods. Safe water saves lives.

Disease Statistics

Over 2 billion people use contaminated water sources globally. 829,000 deaths annually from unsafe water.

Common Pathogens

E. coli, Cholera, Typhoid, Giardia, Hepatitis A - all detectable through water quality testing.

Frequently Asked Questions
What are the first signs of waterborne diseases?
Common early symptoms include diarrhea, vomiting, abdominal cramps, fever, nausea, and dehydration. Seek medical attention if symptoms persist for more than 24 hours.
How does pH affect drinking water safety?
pH below 6.5 can cause corrosion of pipes releasing metals like lead and copper. pH above 8.5 gives water a bitter taste and reduces disinfection efficiency. Ideal range is 6.5-8.5.
What temperature is dangerous for water storage?
Temperatures between 25°C-40°C (77°F-104°F) promote rapid bacterial growth. Store water in cool places below 20°C when possible, and avoid warm storage for more than 24 hours.
Best home water purification methods?
Boiling (most effective), activated carbon filters, UV purification, ceramic filters, and chlorine tablets. For maximum safety, combine boiling with filtration.
